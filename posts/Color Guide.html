Colors To Set Ourselves Apart

... kind of...

Having a thoughful color guide defining a consistent color scheme is helpful for a plethora of reasons but two important reasons are:

  1. Branding yourself
  2. Making sure your content is readable
Branding yourself is important to be recognizable. When someone goes to your website it should look like your website, there should be no confusing websites.

Making your content is readble allows visitors to your website to, well, read your content. You brought them to your website for one reason or another so make it easy for them to look around.

Color Contrast

Avoid red/blue or red/green contrasts; be careful with red in general, as it can impart a negative or alarming meaning to a chart.For accessibility, vary colors by light/dark and by warm/cool tones -- for instance, avoid using gray and a cool-toned color (blue, green, purple) of the same brightness.

Pie Charts

Pie charts should be used (sparingly) to show how various parts make up a whole.Use gradients to show intensity (dark = "Very satisfied" while light = "somewhat satisfied"), colors for other categories (cities, states)Use red sparingly - i.e. to accent one of two categories. (i.e. D.C. vs rest of U.S.)

Line Charts and Scatter Plots

Use a line chart when there is a trend to show or continus data, use a scatter plot when there are data points.
